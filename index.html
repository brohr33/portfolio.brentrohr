02 — Selected Work

Proof, not promises

01 platformOS

Director of Platform Product · AI-native product operating system · Ryan (Tax.com) · 2025–present

Problem Every product org has a knowledge base nobody trusts. Strategy, roadmaps, and engineering artifacts live in separate tools and drift out of sync, so alignment happens in meetings instead of in the work.

What I did Built platformOS, a team-wide knowledge graph that became the single source of truth for how we build product. It links Azure DevOps, Jira, GitHub, and Aha into a living web of strategy, context, and engineering artifacts that our 15-person platform org commits to and queries. The graph generates the technical plans behind story creation, which turned traditional agile into an AI-SDLC and collapsed recurring grooming, refinement, and planning ceremonies into graph-generated plans. Now/next/later roadmaps stay in sync automatically, from executive presentations down to end-of-week reviews. It started at ModuleQ linking commits, stories, and discovery artifacts; at Ryan I took it further into a full knowledge graph.

02 ModuleQ

Director of Product · AI insights for investment banking · 2024–2025

Problem Investment bankers drown in information and neglect their CRM. Insights arrive too late to shape the deal, and every "AI assistant" waits to be prompted.

What I did Co-led AI innovation with the data science team. We built unprompted, personalized workflows in Microsoft Teams using LLMs, embeddings, and agentic RAG. I ran innovation sessions with senior bankers to find the frictions worth solving, then converted proof-of-value pilots into enterprise wins across three Tier-1 GSIB banks. I also stood up LLMOps with Langfuse for observability, prompt management, and tracing, validated against a gold-standard eval set to catch drift before it reached global banking users.

Player-coach. By choice.

I'm a product leader who never stopped building. I started as a software engineer, moved through UX research and product design, then product management, then leading product orgs. I've sat in every seat on a product team, so I relate to everyone on it and I can get in the weeds when it counts.

Lately my focus is enterprise AI. At ModuleQ I co-led AI innovation that shipped LLM, embedding, and agentic RAG workflows to three Tier-1 investment banks, the kind of customer that scrutinizes everything.
